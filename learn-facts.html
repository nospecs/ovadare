High-Risk Factors
High-Risk Factors
High-Risk Factors

When individuals are highly vulnerable, they can more easily be taken advantage of, manipulated, and controlled. Extreme poverty and a lack of economic opportunity are chief factors for most victims. Additionally, traffickers target those who are on the margins of society - those who are perhaps isolated, uneducated, or without strong support systems. This could look like a teenage runaway, a refugee family, or a young person who accepts a job in a new city.

Impoverished / Abused / Neglected / Runaways / Homeless / Victims of social discrimination, domestic violence, trauma or sexual abuse / Victims of war & conflict or fleeing natural disasters / Foreign nationals / Uneducated / Unemployed / Isolated from Society

Traffickers

Traffickers

72% of convicted traffickers are male. 28% are female.

Traffickers could be individuals running their own operation or working as part of an extensive criminal network. They could be part of a gang or a small business owner—anyone willing to exploit another human being for profit.

CONTROL
TACTICS     

Sexual assault / Threats of violence / Isolation / Manipulation and shaming / Drugs and alcohol / Emotional, physical and psychological control / Emotional or physical abuse / Restraining movement by confiscating passports, identification, and money / False documentation

It’s common for traffickers to share the same ethnic and/or cultural background as their victims. This helps them to easily identify vulnerabilities so they can exploit them. Traffickers also understand the psychology of the poor, the desperate, and the young. Preying on their victims’ insecurities and circumstances, traffickers realize an inherent need -- such as money, love, acceptance, food, work, transportation, or lodging -- and offer to meet it. A trafficker may propose an attractive job offer to someone who desperately needs work, pose as an affectionate boyfriend for someone who feels lost or unloved or act as a father figure for a fatherless child.

The Signs Of Trafficking
Abuse

• Physical (marks, bruises, tattoos on body) • Malnourishment • Verbal and emotional abuse • Exhaustion

Duress

• Stress • Panic or fear • Depression and/or suicidal thoughts • Vacant stare

Control

• Limited movement • No official documents • Older perpetrator, younger victim • Few or no personal possessions • Not allowed to speak for oneself

Working and Living Conditions

• Not paid for work, paid very little, or only paid through tips • Works unusual or excessively long hours • Not allowed breaks or has unusual restrictions at work • Unsanitary, poor living conditions according to cultural norms

Signs in Students

• Unexplained school absences • Abrupt change in attire, behavior, or relationships • Presence of an older “boyfriend” or “girlfriend” • Travel with an older male who is not a guardian • Sudden presence of expensive material possessions • Chronic running away

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a connection with slave labor and my stuff?

Yes! The top industries that utilize slave labor are technology (laptops, computers, phones), clothing/accessories, fish, cocoa, timber.

Is there trafficking in the USA?

The “Land of the Free” still has modern slavery. Estimates report over 400,000 victims today in forced labor, forced sexual exploitation, and forced marriage.

Are men and boys affected by human trafficking?

While the majority of human trafficking victims are female, men and boys are also modern-day slaves. They are primarily trafficked for labor but can be trafficked for sex, as well.

What's the difference between prostitution & sex trafficking?

Sex trafficking occurs when either of the following takes place:

  • The person providing sex services is under the age of 18, regardless of the circumstances, and/or
  • An element of fraud, force, coercion, abduction, or threat exists making it difficult for the victim to walk away from the situation.

Prostitution occurs when an individual knowingly and willingly provides sexual services in exchange for payment of some kind.
